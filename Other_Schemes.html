1. NSAP - Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme


One of the five sub-schemes of the National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) is the "Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (IGNDPS)". BPL individuals with severe and multiple impairments who are between the ages of 18 and 59 are eligible to apply under the IGNDPS. The recipient receives a 200 yen monthly pension.

3. Post Matric Scholarship For Minorities


A scholarship programme run by the Ministry of Minority Affairs for members of underrepresented groups enrolling in post-secondary programmes at recognised institutions in India, including residential government institutions and eligible private institutions that have been transparently chosen and notified by the State Government and Union Territory Administration concerned. Additionally, grades XI and XII level technical and vocational courses, including polytechnics and other courses, will be covered in Industrial Training Institutes/ Industrial Training Centres accredited with the National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT).

4. Stand-Up India


An initiative by the Ministry of Finance to support SC/ST and/or female entrepreneurs by enabling bank loans for the establishment of new businesses in the manufacturing, service, trading, etc. sectors. This program's goal is to make bank loans between Rs. 10 lakh and Rs. 1 crore available to at least one SC or ST borrower, as well as one woman borrower, per bank branch for the purpose of starting a new, greenfield business. This company could operate in the manufacturing, service, or trading industries. In the event of non-individual businesses, a SC/ST or woman entrepreneur must possess at least 51% of the stock and the controlling interest.

5. Pragati Scholarship Scheme For Girl Students


An AICTE and MoE scholarship programme for female students enrolled in the first or second year of a technical degree programme at an institution that has received AICTE approval. A family may receive benefits for a maximum of two girl children. For first-year admitted students, the advantages are offered for a maximum of four years, and for second-year admitted students who enter through lateral entry, the benefits are offered for a maximum of three years. This programme aims to empower women by providing them with the knowledge, skills, and self-assurance they need to pursue further education and get ready for prosperous futures.

6. National Overseas Scholarship For Students With Disabilities


A scholarship programme run by the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment for regular, full-time disabled students who wish to pursue advanced study, such as master's or doctoral programmes at foreign universities, in one of the designated fields of study. The "Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016" Schedule defines a list of certain disabilities that qualify pupils for this programme. This includes people who have physical, mental, or developmental problems, as well as those who are deaf or hard of hearing. The DEPwD implements NOS offline.

7. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi


In order to maintain optimal crop health and suitable yields, consistent with the anticipated farm income as well as for home necessities, the scheme intends to complement the financial needs of all landholding farmer families. The Central Government, subject to some exclusions, releases a sum of Rs. 6000/- per year via the internet in the Direct Benefit Transfer manner into the bank accounts of the qualified farmers.

8. Arogya Lakshmi


Through Anganwadi centres, the Telangana government offers one healthy meal each day to pregnant and breastfeeding mothers as well as kids under the age of six. The programme officially began on January 1, 2015. This initiative is run by the Telangana Department of Women's Development and Child Welfare. The Arogya Lakshmi Scheme has been introduced by the Telangana government. Under this programme, pregnant and nursing women receive one complete meal at the Anganwadi centre in addition to iron and folic acid supplements. This plan ensures that the meal will be distributed specifically. This programme was introduced by the Telangana government on January 1st, 2015. The state's 4076 micro Anganwadi centres and 31897 big Anganwadi centres will be used to carry out this programme.

9. Kisan Pension Yojana


The Uttarakhand Pension Yojana's major goal is to give financial aid to all of the state's needy residents. All qualified inhabitants of Uttarakhand will be able to properly maintain themselves through this programme, and their quality of life will also be improved. Through the Uttarakhand Pension Yojana, citizens of Uttarakhand will become independent. Now that this programme is in place, the recipients won't experience any living issues. Direct bank transfers are used to deposit funds into the beneficiary's account. The farmer must be older than 60 in order to benefit from this programme. The annual amount of this cash aid is Rs 14,400. It is given by the government over the course of two installments spaced six to eight months apart.
