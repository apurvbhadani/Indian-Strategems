1. Merit-cum-Means Scholarship For Undergraduate Studies In Various Branches Of Agriculture And Animal Science Subjects


The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), Department of Agricultural Research and Education (DARE), has introduced the "Merit-Means Scholarships For Under Graduate Studies In Various Branches Of Agriculture And Animal Science Subjects" scholarship programme in an effort to attract deserving undergrads to pursue their studies in agriculture, agricultural engineering, home science, dairy, and animal husbandry up until graduation. Each year, scholarships are given out based on merit plus other factors.

2. Pre Matric Scholarship For Minorities


A scholarship programme run by the Ministry of Minority Affairs for minority students in India's first through tenth grades enrolled in public or private schools, including residential government institutions and eligible private schools that have been transparently chosen and announced by the concerned State Government and Union Territory Administration. In addition to renewal scholarships, a total of thirty (30) lakh scholarships are intended to be granted as "Fresh" Scholarships.

3. CBSE Merit Scholarship Scheme For Single Girl Child


The Ministry of Education has a scholarship programme for deserving single female students who are the only child of their parents, completed the CBSE Class 10th Examination with at least 60%, and are pursuing their secondary education in Classes 11 and 12. The programme aims to promote deserving students and reward parents for their efforts in encouraging girls to pursue an education. The monthly scholarship payment will be 500. A scholarship granted under the programme may be paid for up to two years. ECS/NEFT will be used for payment. During the academic year, the tuition charge cannot be more than 1,500 each month in order to be taken into consideration.

4. Support To Students For Participating In Competition Abroad


A travel grant programme run by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), Ministry of Education (MoE), to encourage students to engage in research, innovation, and competition while improving the quality of engineering education nationwide. The programme would offer qualifying students financial aid in the amount of Rs. 1,000,000 as a package, inclusive of all relevant costs, without placing any restrictions on the head of expenditure. The program's goal is to give technical education students financial support so they can participate in international scientific competitions. The program's goal is to pay the registration fees for a team of at least two and up to ten students travelling to international contests in order to support their participation.

5. AICTE – Saksham Scholarship Scheme For Specially-Abled Student (Diploma)


AICTE carries out the Ministry of Education's Saksham Scholarship Programme, which encourages and supports children with disabilities to pursue technical education. This is an effort to provide every young person who is otherwise specially abled with the chance to continue their education and get the technical training/knowledge they need to prepare for a successful future. $50,000 as a lump sum payment towards the payment of college fees, the purchase of computers, stationery, books, equipment, software, etc., for each year of study, up to three years for first-year admitted students and up to two years for second-year admitted students through lateral entry. The applicant should be permitted to enrol in either the first year of the diploma-level course or the second year of the diploma-level course.

6. Post Matric Scholarship For Minorities


A scholarship programme run by the Ministry of Minority Affairs for members of underrepresented groups enrolling in post-secondary programmes at recognised institutions in India, including residential government institutions and eligible private institutions that have been transparently chosen and notified by the State Government and Union Territory Administration concerned.Additionally, grades XI and XII level technical and vocational courses, including polytechnics and other courses, will be covered in Industrial Training Institutes/ Industrial Training Centres accredited with the National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT). In addition to renewal scholarships, a total of five lakh scholarships are intended to be given out as "Fresh". Scholarships. Students with at least 50% in their previous semester's grades will be given scholarships.

7. Post Graduate Merit Scholarship Scheme For University Rank Holders At Under-Graduate Level Applicable


A Post-Graduate Merit Scholarship Scheme was launched by the University Grants Commission (UGC), Department of Higher Education (DoHE), for undergraduate students who placed first in both general and honours courses. For the undergraduate rank winners of each university to study their Master's Degree, the scholarship may be tenable for a period of two years. In both General and Honours Courses, specifically B.A., B.Sc., and B.Com., all universities and degree-awarding institutions will be required to offer rank certificates to the top university students (not at the college level). Along with enhancing the foundational topics, General and Honours at the Undergraduate level, this programme aims to draw talent for postgraduate study.

8. Madhyamik Drop-out Coaching


The Tribal Welfare Department, Directorate of Tribal Welfare, Government of Tripura, introduced the "Madhyamik Drop-out Coaching" programme. The program's goal is to offer coaching to Madhyamik ST students who failed for a variety of reasons or inconveniences and were unable to succeed. Through NGO-run coaching institutions, special instruction is being given to ST pupils who failed the Madhyamik exam for a duration of 7 (seven) months. Hostelers receive a daily stipend of Rs. 65 per student.

9. “Ishan Uday” Special Scholarship Scheme For North Eastern Region


A scholarship programme run by the University Grants Commission and the Ministry of Education. The UGC has decided to launch the "Ishan Uday" Special Scholarship Scheme for North Eastern Region from the academic session 2014–15. This initiative is intended to improve the GER, promote higher education, and inspire children from the economically disadvantaged sections of the NE region. The North Eastern Council, Shillong, the Ministry of Development of North Eastern area, the State Governments and Central & State Universities in the NE area, and other stakeholders were all extensively consulted prior to the start of the programme by the UGC. As confirmed by the university, institution, or college, the scholar would begin receiving his or her award on the day they entered the course.

10. Savitribai Jyotirao Phule Fellowship For Single Girl Child


The University Grants Commission (UGC), Department of Higher Education, offers the Savitribai Jyotirao Phule Fellowship for Single Girl Child (SJSGC) as a Fellowship Programme for conducting research leading to the award of a Ph.D. The "single girl child," or the sole girl in the household without a brother or sister, is the program's target demographic. An applicant for a fellowship under the programme who is a girl scholar and one of the twin daughters or fraternal daughters is also eligible. Every year, the number of fellowship places will be determined based on the number of eligible, fully completed applications received online.

11. Internship Programme By The Ministry Of External Affairs


In 2022, the Government of India's Ministry of External Affairs introduced the first iteration of its internship programme. The internship policy seeks to improve gender inclusion, boost diversity in terms of educational background, place of residence, and socioeconomic position among the cohort of interns hired by the MEA, and bring foreign policy closer to the general public. The interns will learn about a variety of aspects of how the Ministry functions, including its attached offices, public outreach efforts, and role in assisting Indian citizens. Depending on the circumstances, they may also travel to one of the Missions abroad.

12. Revised Education Scheme For Children Of Artisans/Weavers Of Handicrafts And Handloom Departments


An initiative of the Industries and Commerce Department of the Government of Jammu and Kashmir to support financially the completion of education for the children of artisans and weavers in the handicrafts and handloom departments. The scholarship will be given to up to two of the weaver/artisan's children. The student will receive the cash via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). 1500 per year for elementary education; 2000 per year for grades 6 through 10; 3000 per year for grades 11 and 12; and, for technical and professional courses, the actual course cost set by the government.

The Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) scheme of the University Grants Commission (UGC) is open to candidates who qualify for the National Eligibility Test-Junior Research Fellowship(NET-JRF) of the UGC and the UGC-Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (UGC-CSIR) joint test. However, please note that these are qualifying tests only and do not bestow a fellowship upon the candidate.

15. Innovation In Science Pursuit For Inspired Research (INSPIRE) – Fellowship


INSPIRE Fellowship is one of the components of INSPIRE scheme implemented by the Department of Science and Technology (DST), Government of India to strengthen the Science and Technology base of the country. This Component focuses on attracting students to pursue doctoral degrees in basic and applied sciences including engineering, medicine, agriculture, veterinary, pharmacy, etc. after either master’s degree in science/engineering or a bachelor/ master’s degree in Medicine.
